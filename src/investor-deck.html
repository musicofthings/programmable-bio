Problem

India & APAC are invisible on the autonomous lab map

The 2026 autonomous lab revolution runs through Boston, London, and SF — while the world's most diverse patient populations and deepest R&D cost arbitrage sit in India and APAC.

  • Experimental data generation — not ideas — remains biology's rate-limiting step
  • US platforms (Ginkgo, Recursion, Isomorphic) have zero meaningful India/APAC presence
  • Indian CROs execute well but lack an AI orchestration layer for closed-loop discovery
  • Indian genomics players are diagnostics-focused — no full-stack discovery + wet-lab loop
  • 11,000+ biotech startups in India with no indigenous agentic lab platform at scale

Solution

LabOS — we own the experimental graph, not the robots

Discovery-as-a-Service: a multi-agent mesh orchestrating multiomics computation, partner wet labs, and Apollo's clinical biobank into closed-loop DBTL cycles.

  • Experimental graph — every datapoint cryptographically tied to the typed chain of operations that produced it
  • Proprietary clinical data — Apollo Hospitals biobank + pan-India EHR substrate
  • Agent mesh — natural-language hypotheses → auditable wet-lab execution across heterogeneous partners
  • Software margins — partner CRO execution in Phase 1; no $50M+ FAL CapEx
